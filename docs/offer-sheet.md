# Own your obituaries. Own the traffic.

**A searchable, search-engine-ranking obituary section for your newsroom — on your domain, in your brand, generating its own sponsorship revenue.**

---

## The problem

When someone searches **"[name] obituary [your town]"**, they land on Legacy.com — not on you. Your batch obituary posts bury each person, so the individual searches that families and friends actually make go to a national site that keeps the traffic, the ad revenue, and the ranking.

## What you get

A companion to how you already publish — **nothing changes about your newsroom workflow.**

- **One individually-addressable page per person**, built to rank: the name in the title and headline, schema.org structured data, canonical tags, a sitemap, and social share cards. These are what win the searches.
- **A searchable memorial register** embedded on your site — browse by name, month, or funeral home.
- **Permanent pages.** Once published, an obituary's page never disappears, so it keeps ranking for years.
- **A built-in "Submit an obituary" form** so families and funeral homes can send notices directly — or we pull from your existing posts automatically. Your choice.
- **Sponsorable by design.** The section carries a sponsor lockup on every page (think a local funeral home or two). For most newsrooms, **one sponsor covers the entire cost** — and the rest is yours.

Quietly powering **Wausau Pilot & Review** today.
